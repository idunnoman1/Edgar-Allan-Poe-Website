Edgar Allan Poe

Edgar Allan Poe portrait
  • Edgar Allan Poe was born in January 19, 1809.
  • He was an orphan, his biological parents died when he was only 4
  • His first story "Metzengerstein" was released on January 14 1832.
  • Edgar Allan Poe's most famous story is "The Tell-Tale Heart" and it was released in January 1843.
  • His most popular poem is called The Raven and it was published on January 29, 1845.
  • Poe's longest story is called The Narrative of Arthur Gordon Pym of Nantucket and it was a word count of around 72,000 words!
  • His shortest story is The Oval Portrait and only has a little over 1000 words.
  • Poe died on October 7, 1849 when he was 40 years old.
  • He had a large influence on modern day writing, being the one credited for inventing both the horror genre and the detective story, starting with his story called "The Murders in the Rue Morgue". "The Tell-Tale Heart" is his most popular horror story and it cemented his as the "master of the macabre".
